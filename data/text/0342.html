The Group Head Translational Medicine is responsible for clinical development and translational medicine activities aligned with the vision and strategy for the pRED Oncology pipeline and with the Discovery and Translational Area (DTA) priorities.

As the Group Head Translational Medicine, you will report directly to the Global Head of Translational Medicine. You will be the senior scientific and drug development leader and line manager for a group of approximately 8 – 12 Translational Medicine Experts located at the Roche Innovation Center New York.

Roles represented in this team include Translational Medicine Leaders, Clinical Scientists, Clinical Science Specialists and Translational Scientists. The majority of your team members have MD and/or PhD backgrounds.

Your Translational Medicine Team is responsible for the design, clinical strategy, and execution of translational medicine plans for projects in the discovery and early clinical development space, from entry into the discovery portfolio to transition to the late phase development group.

Roche New York is positioned as a premier partner focused on accessing emerging science from US-based biotechnology companies and academic institutions. The NY team combines strong pharma expertise with the agility often associated with small biotech.

Your responsibilities include:

* As a member of the Translational Medicine (TM) Leadership Team, collaborate with peer TM Group Heads and the Biomarker Group Head, who lead clinical and technical TM team members and are responsible for the clinical and scientific deliverables from clinical, pathology, imaging and biomarker teams on pRED Oncology projects

* Provide Scientific Expertise

* Contribute up-to-date translational and drug development expertise

* Ensure strong rationale and justification for entry into humans and/or clinical program based upon data and science

* Require rigorous scientific evaluation

* Impact clinical and discovery projects by supporting your team in real-time exploration and forward/reverse translation of clinical data

* Guide clinical expert team

* Enable proof-of-concept and effective decision making, based on a deep understanding of disease biology and program data

* Provide expert guidance to teams on clinical development plans

* Ensure timely execution against development plans

* Review and provide input in project related documentation for internal and external presentations and submissions

* Support preparation for and attend governance interactions

* Proactively anticipate issues

* Support discovery project teams

* Provide input to discovery teams on unmet medical needs and early clinical development plans

* Contribute translational medicine perspective to discovery pipeline strategy

* Contribute to partnering strategy and projects

* Champion and/or prioritize molecules for due diligence

* Shape the Oncology partnering strategy

* Support clinical expert team members in their contributions to due diligence teams

* Represent your team and demonstrate accountability and decision making for deliverables to teams within pRED and stakeholders across Roche

* Strengthen visibility for the Translational Medicine Team with partner organizations, such as other pRED therapeutic areas, Pharmaceutical Sciences and Clinical Development teams within pRED, and other Research and Early Development as well as the Late Phase (Pharma Development) and Diagnostics organizations

* Ensure consistent partnerships and collaborations with technology platforms and functions, including the pRED Oncology pathology team

* As leader of the New York group of the DTA represent pRED Oncology pipeline and scientific activities in external interactions with US based clinical collaborators and

* Ensure that your clinical expert team is appropriately staffed and effectively uses team resources

* Empower team members and enhance matrix leadership culture

* Create an attractive and inspiring environment in order to attract and retain talent

* Develop internal talent by creating and providing opportunities for project responsibilities, matrix leadership and people management

* Demonstrate and model interest in training, mentoring and coaching senior professionals

* Support team members and follow up on performance

* Provide timely feedback and help generate solutions

* Delegate tasks appropriately

* Live the Roche values (integrity, courage and passion) and the pRED guiding principles (patient focus, valuing talent, truth seeking, innovation, collaboration, continuous improvement, stakeholder engagement)

* Who you are

You're someone who wants to influence your own development. You're looking for a company where you have the opportunity to pursue your interests across functions and geographies. Where a job title is not considered the final definition of who you are, but the starting point.

* MD, board certification and relevant clinical experience in Oncology and/or Hematology

* At least 15 years of professional experience in cancer research, with a minimum of 10 years in biotech or the Pharmaceutical drug development industry. Experience in a global organization and matrix environment is preferred.

* State of the art expertise in the early clinical development of molecularly targeted therapeutics and cancer immunotherapy, documented by deliverables on several past project and/or molecule strategies

* Experience in Late Phase clinical development is a plus

* Excellence in clinical cancer biology research, with a strong publication track-record and reputation

* Proven strong management and interpersonal skills, demonstrated line- and matrix-leadership experience having managed a team of senior experts in the industry environment

* Experience in collaborating with teams across geographic boundaries

* Excellent communication capabilities, collaborative behaviors, and motivational skills

* Who we are

At Roche, 91,700 people across 100 countries are pushing back the frontiers of healthcare. Working together, we've become one of the world's leading research-focused healthcare groups. Our success is built on innovation, curiosity and diversity.
