As the global leader in supercomputing, Cray provides highly advanced supercomputing systems and world-class services and support to government, industry and academia. These systems collect telemetry and other operational data to provide insights into machine operations. The person in this role will develop analytic models and tools to generate operational insights from this data.

Responsibilities:

* Develop models which extract meaningful business insights from the operational data collected from Cray systems.

* Suggest new data sources which could improve our ability to generate operationally helpful models.

* Conduct research and exploratory analyses to uncover potentially useful data trends which will provide insight into Cray systems operations.

* Communicate internally and externally about the insights generated from these models and analysis.Requirements- A minimum of a Master's degree, or equivalent, in a technical field such as engineering, computer science, or mathematics.

* Analytic problem-solving ability.

* Experience working with large data sets on big data platforms like Hadoop and Spark.

* Experience modeling and analyzing streaming time series data strongly preferred.

* Fluency in at least one of Python, Spark, Scala, R and practical experience with Pandas, scikit-learn or equivalent tools.

* Strong theoretical and practical knowledge of foundational statistics concepts and ML algorithms such as linear/logistic regression, random forest, boosting, GBM, NNs, deep learning, etc.

* Ability to access, transform, integrate and analyze complex datasets, in SQL and NoSQL databases.

* Ability to support and collaborate with external partners.

* Ability to explain technology to a wide scope of audience.

* Understanding of commercial frameworks.

* Good judgment in selecting methods and techniques for driving to insights. Work requires imaginative, thorough, and practical solutions to a wide range of problems.

* Preferred in Seattle WA or St. Paul MN.

Keywords: data scientist, big data, analytics, statistical analysis, predictive modeling, optimization, statistics, advanced analytics, machine learning.
