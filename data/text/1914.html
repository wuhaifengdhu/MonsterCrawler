Program Description:

The Data Science and Information Technology Program (DSITP) is composed of five functional areas: 1) the Advanced Biomedical Computing Center (ABCC) provides scientific and bioinformatics support, 2) the CBIIT Technical Support Group (CTSG) provides integrated program management, operational, development, and analysis support for major NCI initiatives sponsored through the Center for Bioinformatics and Information (CBIIT), 3) the IT Operations Group (ITOG) serves as the primary provider and maintainer of cyberinfrastructure for the Frederick National Laboratory for Cancer Research (FNLCR) and NCI-Frederick, 4) the Information Security and Compliance Office (ISCO) oversees data and network security and privacy for systems connected to the NCI-Frederick network and subcontracted information systems sponsored through FNLCR, and 5) the Program Administration and Operations (PAO) Office oversees DSITP's business operations and contract deliverables. Additionally, DSITP manages the execution of IT developmental, IT infrastructure maintenance, data science, informatics, and scientific projects for the National Cancer Institute, a $20M+ portfolio.

The DSITP Project Management Office (PMO) has a group of project managers whose mission is to fully integrate Leidos Biomed project management practices at the project level and to provide intake and oversight, monitoring and support to the portfolio of projects within DSITP. The group is responsible for providing visibility across all DSITP projects, using effective communication, reporting and clearly established expectations and metrics throughout the project lifecycle.

Job Description/Basic Function: THIS POSITION IS CONTINGENT UPON FUNDING APPROVAL

Leidos Biomedical Research Inc. is seeking a Technical Project Manager to work in the PMO ensuring that projects make disciplined use of well-defined project management and communication plans and appropriate mechanisms and controls for early detection and reporting of at‐risk projects.

* Leads and directs one or more project teams engaged in the design, implementation and modification of IT projects that span across functional organizations

* Prepares project execution plans and identifies appropriate technical and business resources needed for the team

* Ensures projects are completed on schedule, within budget, and meet technical and scope requirements of the assignment

* Provides technical and analytical guidance to project team

* Interfaces with stakeholders to ensure requirements are met

* May Serves as an in-house project consultant for evaluating needs and vendor requests for proposals- May build tools/products for company internal use

The successful candidate will 1) manage a portfolio of projects within the Data Science and Information Technology Program from inception to delivery, 2) be responsible for liaison activities within the enterprise and maintain close coordination with DSITP personnel, and 3) facilitate and coordinate with other groups within NCI and Leidos Biomed.

Qualifications:

BASIC QUALIFICATIONS:

To be considered for this position, you must minimally meet the knowledge, skills, and abilities listed below:

* Possession of a Master's degree from an accredited college or university.

* Foreign degrees must be evaluated for U.S. equivalency.

* Must be able to obtain and maintain a Public Trust Clearance

* Minimum of ten (10) years progressively responsible job related experience

* Strong project management skills

* Demonstrated success in managing technical projects including the abilities to 1) initiate and plan a project, execute, control and monitor and close out a project, 2) develop a technical proposal with schedule and budget, 3) execute on a project with continuous risk assessment and mitigation, schedule and budget management, and status reporting.

* Exceptional written and oral communication skills to work closely with numerous stakeholders from diverse organizations

* Ability to prioritize multiple tasks and projects

* Functions well as a member of a larger team

* Excellent problem solving and project risk assessment skills

PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS:

Candidates with these desired skills will be given preferential consideration:

* Project management experience in a government contracting environment

* Knowledge of formal continuous improvement methodologies

* Project Management Professional certification is a plus
