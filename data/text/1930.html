The System Administrator for the Homewood High Performance Cluster (HHPC) manages aresearch computing cluster, which contains over 3,000 processor cores and is connected to apetabyte of storage that serves the HPC and data intensive science needs of many researchers acrossthe Homewood campus. The System Administrator oversees systems management for the group'sfileservers, cluster login nodes, and associated switching fabrics and networks, and implements andmanages the queuing system that balances usage among groups.

Essential Duties & Responsibilities:

Systems Engineering and Oversight

* Provides stable solutions for HHPC use

* Oversee maintenance of HHPC community's technical infrastructure

* Maintain HHPC and Related clusters

* Plans and makes purchases to meet the needs of the HPC community.

* Maintain job scheduling and storage allocation systems and policies to ensure fair allocation ofshared resources.

* Maintain extensive monitoring systems to facilitate quick, proactive responses to routine failures,and to provide comprehensive performance data logging.

* Oversee purchasing of additions to existing clusters.

* Maintain power and cooling information about data centers and server rooms run by the ResearchCenter.

* Consult with and provide expertise to building engineers and other staff on new Research Data

* Assist with facilities-related issues that directly affect the Research Data Center (Bloomberg 156).

* Provide required facility activity data for University and government reports.

* May provide general system administration backup for other facilities or research groups.
