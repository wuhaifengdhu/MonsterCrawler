Job Title Executive Director, Student Science
City Baltimore
Institution Laureate Education, Inc.
External Job Description As a member of the LNO Student Success team, the Executive Director, Student Science is responsible for establishing the data science vision, strategy, and best practices for the LNO Student Success team. This leader will consult on practical applications for improving customer outcomes across the network, working closely with partner institutions in adoption of evidence-based best practices.
ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES:
  • Lead and facilitate discussion and needs analysis globally and regionally with senior executives and key stakeholders for student success efforts and initiatives for each Laureate International University.
  • Drive revenue, reputation and thought leadership for LNO and the Laureate network of universities through the application of cognitive-behavioral insights.
  • Manage a group of external advisors in relevant cognitive and behavioral science areas
  • Lead the conceptual design and deployment of a Laureate proprietary student behavioral cognitive profile model supporting retention efforts, service optimization, and personalization.
  • Lead, collaborate and work with local universities and key contacts in assembling, updating, and reporting student success initiatives for Laureate universities
  • Design and execute research interventions towards improving Retention, Student Engagement and Employability outcomes for Laureate.
  • Contribute to the productization of Retention products with focus on scaleability, institutional fit and efficiency across the network. (e.g., Customer Effort Score, Customer Profile, Early Success Toolkit)
  • Drive cross-functional collaboration with global institution stakeholders to ensure clarity in project goals and alignment of priorities, timelines and objectives.
  • Develop the process for systematically testing new innovations towards improving the customer experience.
  • Collaborate with institutions and influence leaders and stakeholders to develop their retention strategy, based on maturity and modality
  • Maintain close awareness of and contact with leading edge research, products, and services relative to student success;
  • Collaborate with LNO operations in the areas of MBI (Predictive Analytics) to deliver product to clients effectively and on a timely manner
  • Responsible for cross-functional management of global project teams dedicated to the execution of retention strategy within research pilots
  • Drive student success findings in to key areas beyond student services such as enrollment, curriculum, faculty development and management.
  • Responsible for collecting, consolidating and reporting key pilot data to LNO & LIU management, – applying  behavioral science insights to assist network decision making
External Requirements
PROBLEM SOLVING
The focus of this role is to apply cutting-edge knowledge and doctoral-level skills in behavior and cognitive science to solve key problems in student success –  retention, graduation, and career outcomes – and to lead the incorporation of the solutions into products that will serve Laureate’s global network of institutions and students.  Specifically, the incumbent will need to apply the following problem solving skills:
  • Identifying and characterizing opportunities to improve student success
  • Synthesizing Laureate and outside research to develop simple and powerful models of student success that will drive both product development and change management across the network
  • Defining and conducting programs of research to understand Student Success issues more deeply, and to characterize potential solutions in terms of practicality, cost, impact, and change management requirements
  • Working with senior leadership to identify and seize opportunities for Student Success thought leadership for Laureate

TECHNICAL, MANAGERIAL, and PEOPLE SKILLS REQUIRED
To perform this job successfully an individual must be able to perform each essential duty satisfactorily.  The requirements listed below are representative of the knowledge, skill, and/or ability required.  Incumbents will be evaluated, in part, based on performance of each essential function.  Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform essential functions.

EDUCATION and/or EXPERIENCE:

  • PhD in relevant field  required, with minimum 10 years of experience in education research and design
  • Customer service oriented.
  • Attention to detail, habit of working with deadlines, organization and problem-solving skills.
  • Excellent communications skills in written and spoken English, Spanish and Portuguese desirable.
  • Team Player, will work closely with key contacts inside institutions, Directors, Product Managers, Operations Administrators and Shared Services to reach goals and meet deadlines.
  • Marketing and promotion skills
  • Discipline, Energy and Organization.
  • International communication skills.
  • Leadership, supervision and time management skills.
  • Must be self-motivated and demonstrate initiative.
TRAVEL
Occasional travel may be required
