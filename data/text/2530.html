Title Data Scientist
City McLean
State Virginia
Employment Type Full Time
Hot Job No
Description

Infoscitex, a DCS company, is seeking a Data Scientist to support a prototype and Research and Development (R&D) effort. In this role, you will use your specialty in collecting, creating, cleansing, correlating, analyzing, and storing data. This effort will span many components and include a variety of activities, including collecting, tracking, and integrating multiple sources of data.

In this role, you will be working with established programmatic and quantitative methods, find patterns and relationships in large data sets; conduct mathematical, statistical or other data driven problem solving analysis to identify business operations or intelligence questions by internal and external customers. You would work independently with minimal supervision by first line supervisor or government client to design, develop, test and deploy in the customer architecture, Test specific functionality and capability of multiple analytic software tools and algorithms. Independently compile data to address the most complex intelligence issues or problems for customers.

With minimal supervision, you will provide accurate, timely, complex and sophisticated data analysis in support of key Agency, US National security, and foreign policy objectives to shape Intelligence Community (IC) analysis priorities. Conceive, prepare, and communicate a wide range of strategic, highly complex graphics, computational models or tools, or written and oral assessments to internal and external customers (e.g., policymakers, IC components).

Depending on assignment, you may manage projects and associated resources.

Some travel may be required.

Requirements

Due to the sensitivity of customer related requirements, U.S. Citizenship is required.

Applicants selected will be subject to a U.S. Government background investigation and must meet eligibility requirements for access to classified information: TS/SCI.

BS degree in Engineering, Computer Science, Mathematics, or related quantitative or technical discipline.

At least 10 years of related experience.

Working knowledge of appropriate analytic methods and methodological tools in one or more of the following areas:

Applied mathematics (e.g. probability and statistics, formal modeling, computational social sciences)

Computer programming (e.g. programming languages, math/statistics packages, computer science, machine learning, scientific computing).

Visualization experience (e.g.; GIS/Geospatial analysis, telemetry analysis).

Working knowledge of research designs.

Intellectual curiosity: creativity and innovation to go beyond current tools to deliver the best solutions to complex problems.

Strong analytical and critical thinking skills

Ability to leverage multiple data management tools to organize relevant information and make decisions.

Ability to develop comprehensive software applications, as needed.

Ability to translate complex, technical findings into an easily understood narrative.

Ability to work collaboratively and effectively in a team environment.

Ability to travel, as needed.

Familiarity with some of the following Technologies:

Statistical Tools: SAS, R, SPSS, Matlab, Mahout/MADlib, Stata

Programming languages: SQL, C/C, JAVA, Python, Perl

Data Management Tools: Hadoop tools, MapReduce, Pig, Hive, NoSql, Tableau, Hbase
