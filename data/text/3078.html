So urce Throughput Analyst

EXPERT LEVEL

Source Throughput Analysts support the end-to-end process of delivering all source imagery data in a manner to optimize the performance and capabilities of the delivery architecture.Source Throughput Analyst personnel will execute an integrated process to manage and oversee the primary dissemination access and retrieval to appropriate buffers and to the Intelligence Community ITE cloud environment for the exploitation of National space-spaced, airborne, FGI, and commercial data to the requestor site and serve as the primary 24-hour interface for collection and system issues.


Here are the specifics of what you’ll be doing:

  • Monitor and report on network health and status, assist in implementing NGA release policy, monitor site bandwidth utilization, recommend alternative delivery paths, maintain user site profiles/allocation, and support operations integration of new data sources, processes and system capabilities, customer products from collection systems, community libraries, and external sites to support intelligence community needs.
  • Support all aspects of the end to end integration of GEOINT source providers; including CONOPS development, data transport mechanisms, capability descriptions, and tradecraft applications
  • Support the creation, coordination and implementation of complex throughput and dissemination strategies.
  • Support technology integration for Source Operations Group needs
  • Support new tool/technology delivery by coordinating stakeholder engagement and documenting applicable procedures.
  • Conduct thorough analysis of organizational tool/technology needs and monitor internal requests for capabilities through entire life-cycle of acquisition processes
  • Support evaluation and analysis of potential tool capabilities for operational effectiveness
  • Monitor airborne throughput and support troubleshooting efforts
  • Coordinate airborne throughput strategies to ensure end-to-end delivery across NSG and mission partner networks
  • Draft CONOPS and recommend integration strategies for emerging airborne data providers
  • Provide expertise on NTM dissemination strategies in accordance with policy guidance
  • Reviewing customer needs through the use of GIMS to support additions or modification to existing data dissemination profiles
  • Support end to end check out of new NTM data providers in collaboration with NGA and NSG mission partners.
  • Provide virtual and onsite support to IPOC operations
  • Supports the evaluation and assessment with Data Science Analysts and Source Strategy Analysts for proper valuation manifestations (of the value model) and appropriate supplier response to ensure analytic satisfaction based on Intelligence problem KIQ
  • Provide support to the Source Assessment and Integration Working Group (SAIWG) meetings for the integration of all non-programed sensors
  • Monitor and analyze dissemination requirements to ensure efficient and effective use of throughput management systems and assets to meet the needs of the Intelligence Community (IC). In the executionof this task the Contractor may be required to travel to US and International customer sites to develop proper and efficient throughput management profiles and dissemination strategies
  • Participate in releasability of domestic imagery to authorized users and domestic auditing of NGA users
  • Participate in releasability of Limited Dissemination (LIMDIS) imagery to authorized users
  • Work with Portfolio leads to identify future tools and requirements for the exposing collection content
  • Monitor ongoing iterative development of existing National space-based, airborne, FGI, and commercial systems and development of future systems beginning with installation, checkout, and test activities; advise system owners and Contractors on implementation questions
  • Participate in planning and conduct of new National space-based, airborne, FGI, and commercial systems and capability testing beginning with stability and operability and/or transition and operations confidence testing; assist in coordinating Source participation in testing
  • Manage implementation of systems, discovery tools, procedures for new data source integration
  • Monitor operational systems critical to tasking, collection, processing, dissemination, and exploitation of imagery derived from National space-based, airborne, FGI, and commercial systems; advise appropriate organizations of significant relevant issues and facilitate problem resolution as necessary to the Government
  • Work directly with the Source customers to understand mission needs, current bandwidth utilization and system performance in order to advocate any necessary changes to the NSG on their behalf
  • Support the integration of National space-based, airborne, FGI, and commercial production and dissemination requirements and systems into the developing NSG architecture
  • Contribute and participate in throughput management related meetings and working groups to ensure effective integration of processing and dissemination requirements, systems and architecture changes
  • Perform customer outreach visits to address throughput issues affecting the delivery of National space-based, airborne, FGI, and commercial data
  • Participate in the system/segment anomaly investigation process by evaluating potential resolutions and providing recommendations to the Government on the risks and benefits of such resolutions
  • Ensure the proper implementation of the over-arching guidance from NGA’s Office of International and Policy (OIP), for dissemination of National space-based, airborne and commercial imagery to National, Civil, Military, International Programs and any other user site
  • Support the Mission Integration Office in managing the dissemination and throughput of source data through multiple pathways while migrating from a controlled RMS P&D push to a web-based pull environment
  • Provide support to Source and NGA level forums and working groups to develop source management policies and procedures for review by the Source Policy representative.
  • Support the Source Policy representative by working with NGA OGN, OGC, OIP, and external agency’s to update all DNI level Directives relating to throughput management and releaseability
  • Coordinate directly with the customers to understand their mission requirements, and monitor current customer usage and system performance so as to advocate change to the NGA baseline on behalf of the customer community
  • Coordinate with other NGA entities and the Mission Partner to evaluate commercial and airborne P&D imagery requirements and systems’ throughput characteristics into the developing NSG architecture. The Contractor shall monitor status of commercial and airborne systems for throughput issues, investigate problems and facilitate resolution of those problems
  • Provide recommendation to Source on P&D process improvements and the proper steps for implementation. The Contractor shall work with other NGA entities and the mission partner to develop required interfaces, CONOPS documentation, and coordination mechanisms
  • Identify and resolve policy and systems operations issues that impact the ability to effectively manage source throughput operations across the NSG and IC interfaces including reviewing all proposed changes to current and future processes and procedures that affect overall policy, releasability, and Limited Distribution (LIMDIS) management
  • Provide expertise in the current and future operational architecture and its impact on Source’s customer base
  • Provide airborne throughput management analysis of current architecture and use of airborne imagery
  • Assist in the functional management of source airborne operations to include the end-to-end fulfillment of customer’s geospatial intelligence needs
  • Assist in the tracking, monitoring, evaluation, and ordering of commercial imagery, the management of all imagery tasking and accounting functions and in the tracking of all commercial imagery tasks assigned to Source
  • Facilitate Source participation in planning and conducting of commercial systems and capability testing beginning with stability and operability and/or transition and operations confidence testing
  • Consult with diverse groups on requirements performance systems issues; advise management on system issues affecting the performance management process
  • Serve as the liaison to DIA/DCCC, USAF 480 th Intelligence Wing, NGA customers, JFCC-ISR, 2 nd party customers and other IC customers on all airborne imagery throughput matters
  • Prepare and present to the Government new policy guidance and implement new airborne and commercial releasability and classification guidance. The Contractor shall coordinate all security concerns with regard to handling and releasability with other interagency offices and outside agencies. The Contractor shall assist interagency offices in the proper handling of Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) requests
  • Participate in the system anomaly investigation process by evaluating potential resolutions and providing recommendations to the Government on the risks and benefits of such resolutions
  • Assist in the functional management of source airborne operations to include the end-to-end fulfillment of customer’s geospatial intelligence needs
  • Provide subject matter expertise on current and future airborne sensor/platform capabilities and assist in the advancement and integration of airborne operations in Source. The Contractor shall assist in providing guidance, processes, standards, training, education and liaison services to regional division source analysts and airborne cells and shall provide reporting on airborne collection activities
  • Prepare detailed concepts of operations, programmatic plans, standard operating procedures, and provide support to airborne imagery policy development
  • Provide support to Source forums and working groups to develop source management policies and procedures
  • Perform Sensitive Reconnaissance Operations (SRO) liaison activities in support of JCS, US Strategic Command, Joint Functional Component Command-Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance (JFCC-ISR), and NGA. Assist in the coordination of Source Strategies inputs to SRO Book Review and coordinate activities associated with the Airborne and Maritime SRO Schedule Review
  • Assist in coordinating US Strategic Command issued Global Intelligence, Surveillance and Reconnaissance deployment orders shall serve as liaison to regional/Combat Commander Joint Reconnaissance Centers and shall coordinate with JCS, DIA, and USSTRATCOM elements for analysis of airborne requirements
  • Design and document National-Theater-Tactical integration for tasking, requirements management, and throughput management concepts to feed multi-agency centers integrated into the NSG

This is a challenging job, and requires you to bring these skill sets to the table:​

  • 20 years of experience (education and work experience will be considered)
  • Current DOD Top Secret clearance with eligibility for SCI access.
  • Understanding of the National System for Geospatial-Intelligence (NSG) and the data dissemination architecture, policies, and supporting processes from an apprentice to expert level.
  • Understanding of GEOINT collection platforms and sensors designed to assist in GEOINT collection, production and visualization from an apprentice to expert level.
  • Familiarity with the NSG’s Tasking, Collection, Processing, Exploitation, and Dissemination (TCPED) from an apprentice to expert level.
  • Knowledge of NGA’s Enterprise Services Dissemination Processes and supporting architectures.
  • Familiarity with all capabilities and use all GIMS functionalities to include but not limited to Advanced Search, Dissemination Rules, Strategies, NGDS-Discovery Services, GIN Creation, GIN Modification and Approval, Tasking Dashboard, Geospatial Dashboard, Supplier Management, OOI Target Creation, OOI Query, OOI Modification, OOI Management, AGI Search, Virtual File Folders, and UIM-Approved Product Holdings (Report) Search
  • Possess expertise in the current and future operational architecture, review related documents and plans, and provide comments and recommendations regarding potential impacts on Source’s customer base
  • Fully understand how security indicator codes function relative to their protection of sensitive, non-sensitive and domestic data. They will understand how the various dissemination systems use these codes to limit dissemination of data, and how these systems can be overridden to allow 'releasable' products to individuals or organizations
  • Have expertise in executing NGA’s International program, both technically and programmatically
  • Be level one certified as a GEOINT Officer and a level two collection manager.
