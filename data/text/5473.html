POSITION SUMMARY:

Work in this position involves working with OIT's architect team including the Sr. Technical Directory in planning, implementing, and leading teams of engineers and developers in integrating and automating provisioning, monitoring, and maintenance of the broad range of IT services and infrastructure at Duke. The position will work closely with other members of OIT and the campus community at large to refine create new services in support of Duke's research and education mission.

Existing and emerging IT services span a broad range of technologies, and the senior developer must have significant experience in a variety of areas including databases (SQL, MySQL, Neo4J/graph databases, and other emerging database technologies), service integration and delivery technologies (REST and SOAP web service APIs, JSON, Windows, Unix/Linux) and languages (Ruby, Python, Perl, Javascript, Java, Ruby, SQL, C). A fundamental knowledge of standard networking services and operating systems is required. Beyond technical knowledge, the senior developer should have demonstrated the ability to coordinate and lead teams of developers and engineers, work as an effective team member with both technical and non-technical groups, introduce new technology to large organizations, and lead large-scale technical projects to completion.

A pragmatic approach to system development, systems administration, experience working with development teams, good communication skills, and broad exposure to the IT industry (ideally including Higher Education) are vital for this position.

DUTIES & WORK PERFORMED:

The person serving in this role will

* Lead development teams on large scale enterprise-wide projects and mentor junior developers

* Provide DevOps support for research computing projects

* Plan and implement systems in broad range of technologies including JavaScript, REST and SOAP web service APIs, Ruby, Python, PowerShell, bash shell scripting, and PHP on multiple platforms including as Linux and Windows

* Understand and shape the application design/development process, including customer contact, implementation planning, time and effort estimates

* Effectively use existing open source projects and services and current, proprietary systems in use at Duke.

* Efficiently prototype and deploy systems that automate provisioning of a broad array of IT services in support of research computing

* Identify candidates for automation in workflows and routine systems in operation

* Possess proven design and debugging skills

* Write end-user documentation for non-technical staff and bridge the gap between systems engineers, network engineers, software developers and automation engineers.

* Interact with developers, researchers, and staff inside of OIT as well as from University and Health System departments such as TTS, DHTS, and ORI.

Develop, integrate, and deploy applications and web services that leverage web APIs (both RESTful and SOAP based) to provide/automate services and management functions to meet business and research needs at Duke University.

SOFT SKILLS:

The role requires working well as a team member, with people who are technically and computationally astute and who are not, though have knowledge of other areas important to an infrastructure or a scholarly project. Communication skills are essential, both within the team and beyond it, into the Office of Information Technology and the University. The person serving in the role will frequently call upon written and oral communication skills in the conduct of work. SUPERVISORY RESPONSIBILITIES:NoneQUALIFICATIONS:The person serving in the role should have a high level understanding of major components of a large IT enterprise, including concepts of networking, virtualization, storage, monitoring, security, identity management, application development methods, documentation, development of APIs, and workflows relating to provisioning of various IT services. Solid working understanding of "DevOps" methodologies is particularly useful, and familiarity with associated tools including continuous integration and automated deployment systems like Jenkins, Puppet, Ansible, Docker and associated "middleware." The person serving in this role will possess a deep understanding and hands-on experience with specific technologies used in integration and automating provisioning, configuration, and maintenance of whole infrastructures. Expertise in scripting and coding skills in common languages is a requirement. Familiarity with a broad range of scripting and programming languages is also required.

The person serving in the role will possess superior problem solving skills and be able to clarify technical issues relating to a desired outcome in automating IT services of all kinds. The person will have personal qualities that add to the depth of competence and intelligence of the development teams at the Office of Information Technology.

EDUCATION:

Required: BS in a quantitative field such as computer science, statistics, mathematics, data sciences.

Preferred: Advanced degree is desirable. Advanced training in specific areas, technical or not, are an indicator of intellectual ability and curiosity.

EXPERIENCE & SKILLS:

Required: A progressive record of accomplishment in technical and computational areas, including such areas as computational research and development, systems development, systems administration and "DevOps," and/or web and application development.

Demonstrated ability to work effectively in a team environment.

Preferred: Understanding and appreciation of work environments in an academic setting.

WORKING CONDITIONS:Normal office working environment.
