Johnson & Johnson is recruiting for a Lead Specialist, HCC&P North America Testing Operations. The position will be based in Titusville, NJ or New Brunswick, NJ.

Caring for the world, one person at a time, has inspired and united the people of Johnson & Johnson for over 125 years. We embrace research and science -- bringing innovative ideas, products and services to advance the health and well-being of people. Employees of the Johnson & Johnson Family of Companies work with partners in health care to touch the lives of over a billion people every day, throughout the world.

With $70 billion in 2015 sales, Johnson & Johnson is the world's most comprehensive and broadly based manufacturer of health care products, as well as a provider of related services, for the consumer, pharmaceutical, and medical devices and diagnostics markets. There are more than 265 Johnson & Johnson operating companies employing approximately 126,500 people and with products touching the lives of over a billion people every day, throughout the world.

The Lead Specialist, HCC&P (Health Care Compliance & Privacy) North America Testing Operations will be responsible for executing testing, monitoring and program assessments of HCC, GCC (Government Contracting Compliance) and Privacy, covering all sectors (Pharmaceutical, Medical Device, Consumer, Corporate, and Supply Chain) in both The United States and Canada. Activities include end-to-end execution of assessments, and conducting data analytics and data mining as part of the testing and monitoring process. Additionally, the Lead Specialist conducts assessments by attending and observing various types of field sales interactions with Health Care Professionals. Special focus will be directed to integrating newly developed data mining and analytics capabilities into testing, auditing and monitoring processes to enhance oversight, scope and efficiencies. The Lead Specialist reports to the Senior Manager, North America Testing Operations.

Main job tasks and responsibilities include:

* Executes tests and assessments, partnering with assigned compliance officers as needed

* Utilizes tools to perform data mining and analytics, reporting on potential risk areas and identifying opportunities for testing and monitoring

* Communicates results with Company Management and documents results in written reports

* Performs field observations at designated locations throughout North America

* Utilizes internal reporting and accounting systems to access data and conduct analyses

* Produces reports and dashboards for business partners and management consumption

* Executes harmonized and standardized testing protocols, systems and processes in partnership with compliance officers

* Provides compliance officers with information around best practices, if applicable

* Identifies opportunities for improved data collection and process improvement

Some of the key competencies crucial for success in this role include the following: Critical thinking and problem solving skills; Planning and organizing; Communication skills; Influencing and leading; Self-awareness; Team work; Conflict management; Adaptability; and Stress tolerance.

Qualifications

* A minimum of a Bachelor's Degree is required. Area of study in finance, data science, accounting, or similar is preferred.

* Advanced degree, CPA or SCCE training is preferred

* A minimum of 2 years of testing/auditing experience is required

* A minimum of 6 years of experience in HCC or related compliance function (regulatory, quality, finance, GCC, HCC, Privacy), and a minimum of 4 years of experience in the pharmaceutical and/or medical device industries is required.

* Demonstrated technical and analytical capabilities (both quantitative and statistical) is required

* Cross-functional and/or cross-sector experience is preferred

* Knowledge of HCC/fraud and abuse laws, financial or auditing practices, and company business and policies is required

* Advanced Excel skills including an understanding of database structures and experience building reports from complicated datasets is preferred

* Experience with dashboards and BI tools (such as Tableau) is preferred

* Excellent verbal and written communication skills, including formal presentation skills, is required

* Ability to successfully manage multiple critical issues simultaneously is required

* Ability to influence, negotiate and communicate with both internal and external customers is required

* Knowledge of Process Excellence methods and project management is preferred

* This position will be located in Titusville, NJ or New Brunswick, NJ and will require up to 40% of travel, primarily in North America.
